About

I am a Ph.D. student in Operations Research at Purdue University, advised by Prof. Susan R. Hunter. My research focuses on stochastic optimization, set-valued optimization, and the theoretical foundations of multi-stage multi-objective decision-making, with additional interests in reinforcement learning and statistical simulation.

Research Interests

  • Large-scale and stochastic optimization
  • Set-valued and multi-objective optimization
  • Statistical simulation and empirical process theory
  • Reinforcement learning and actor–critic methods

Research Experience

Stochastic Set-valued Optimization (SSO)
Research Assistant, School of Industrial Engineering, Purdue University
Jan 2025 – Present
  • Developing theoretical foundations for two-stage and multi-stage stochastic multi-objective optimization (TSSMOO/MSSMOO), including measurability and integrability of set-valued objectives.
  • Establishing equivalence between multi-stage multi-objective optimization and set-valued optimization, and constructing Sample Average Approximation (SAA) procedures.
  • Extending probabilistic lower bounds of SAA to multidimensional settings using geometric and stochastic process tools (e.g., Hausdorff distance).
  • Additional exploration: Benson’s geometrical duality, Benders decomposition, cutting-plane methods, and Mordukhovich derivatives.
